About

I'm a physicist turned research data engineer, embedded with research teams across disciplines at the University of Sheffield. I design research software, build data pipelines, and steward data through its whole lifecycle — from sensitive NHS health records to environmental sensor streams and genomic sequencing data. I care about FAIR, reproducible workflows, and about using AI and machine learning responsibly in the service of good science.

Before research computing I completed a PhD in physics (atom interferometry) and worked in analytics and software development across financial services. These days I mentor engineers, lead research data projects, and play an active part in the Research Software Engineering community — teaching, writing documentation, and contributing to open source.
